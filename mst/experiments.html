Experiments

In order to define the experiments, we compiled a list of common processing steps to transform the input data. These are listed in the table below. For reference, we also provide the steps used by MIDI-VAE as it was one of the models originally considered but it didn't make the cut.

ChordGAN CycleGAN MIDI-VAE
Time signatures used 4/4 4/4 any
Time steps per bar 16 16 16
Number of bars 1 4 1
Lowest pitch 24 24 24
Highest pitch 102 108 84
Pitch range 78 84 60
Velocity 127 127 127
Instrument tracks all all (merged), no drums top 4 (separate), no drums

The experiments were centered around two of the steps above: sampling rate and number of bars. We hypothesised that, since these two settings control the sequence length of the inputs, they control the amount of information that the model is able to learn from each sample and could have big impact on performance.

Maintaining the model configuration and training settings fixed, ChordGAN was trained on Pop and Jazz data using sampling rates of 8, 16 and 32 and 1, 2, 4, 8 for the number of bars , yielding 24 different models (12 per genre).

CycleGAN, being a larger model, took longer to train and so we limited the experiments to using 1, 2, 4, and 8 bars for inputs, with a total of 12 models being trained, 4 per pair of genres (pop & jazz, pop & classical, jazz & classical).

The training settings and parameters are listed below.

Setting ChordGAN CycleGAN
Optimizer Adam (x2) Adam
Optimizer - Learning Rate 0.0002 0.00001
Optimizer - $\beta$1 0.5 0.9
$\sigma$d 1 --
$\lambda$ 10 100
# of epochs 30 500
