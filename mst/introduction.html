Introduction

The past decade saw Artificial Intelligence go from a niche academic topic and sci-fi staple to a popular phenomenon in the media. As a result, a field that has emerged from the increased research is Image Style Transfer, which aims to convert an input image to the style of another. For example a picture of a dog in the style of Kandinsky.

Style Transfer soon reached the music domain community and Music Style Transfer emerged as a field of research. Both unsupervised and supervised models have been proposed by authors in the field, and while the results obtained show that AI can be used to convert songs to a different genre, the variety in approaches taken and choice of tools used has made comparing the results a difficult task.

This project, done as part of my MSc in Artificial Intelligence course taken at the University of Limerick, is a deep dive into some of the models used in Music Style Transfer and it aimed to:

  • Provide a new codebase for existing Music Style Transfer methods using updated libraries
  • Explore approaches taken to model data for Music Style Transfer
  • Define experiments for preparing the data
  • Train and evaluate selected models for each experiment
  • Benchmark the models using a set of metrics recently proposed in the literature
